### Damage Types
    Dealing critical damage triggers fortitude check upon target of the damage. 
    DC is equal to received damage. On failure, target receives an ailment based on the type of damage.
    Certain effects may specify that it does not need critical damage to trigger an ailment check. 

    Physical: Slashing              / inflicts "bleeding" 
    Physical: Piercing              / inflicts "bleeding" 
    Physical: Bludgeoning           / inflicts "overwhelmed" 
    Physical: Shockwave / Sonic     / inflicts "overwhelmed" and "deafened"
    Energy: Fire                    / inflicts "burning"
    Energy: Cold                    / inflicts "chilled" 
    Energy: Lightning               / inflicts "shocked"
    Energy: Chemical                / inflicts 2 stacks of "weakness" for 1d4 turns.
    Energy: Force                   / no extra effect
    Energy: Radiant                 / inflicts "blinded"
    Bio: Poison                     / inflicts 1 stack of "weakness" until cured
    Bio: Necrotic                   / inflicts "necrosis" for 1d4 turns
    Bio: Psychic                    / inflicts "overwhelmed"
    Bio: Internal                   / no extra effect 

### Damage Prevention
    Armor Class: 
    Base measure of attack damage avoidance. Attacks against a target,
    with Accuracy Check below target's AC miss and do not deal any damage.
    Missed attack may be interpreted as being blocked by shield,
    dodged, or entirely missed by the attacker. 

    Resistance: 
    Halves damage taken from specified damage type (e.g. Piercing Damage Resistance) 
    Gives Advantage on checks against certain effects (e.g. Resistance to Mystic Powers) 

    Immunity
    Ignores a given type of damage taken or status effect (Fire damage Immunity).
    Automatically succeed checks against specified ailment (Immunity to Stunned). 

    Damage Threshold 
    A value which defines minimum damage attacker has to deal to affect target's HP. 
    For example, a character with 10 Energy Damage Threshold will ignore any energy damage lesser than 10. 

    Damage Reduction: 
    A value which reduced damage taken from a source by that value. For example, Prowess Ability "Unyielding", reduces all damage taken by 3.

### Hit Points:
    Hit points dictate damage required to kill or destroy character, item or structure.
    When character reaches 0 HP, it goes unconscious,
    and regains consciousness upon regenerating 20% of max HP.
    If character gets damaged such that they would reach 100% of the max HP below 0,
    they instantly die and lose all regeneration pool. 

    To rest and recover resources, a character can take a short or a long rest. 
    Their duration and requirements are defined by characters race. During a short 
    rest, character recovers 25% + 5% per Constitution modifier of max HP.
    During a long rest, character recover all HP. 

    Health recovery of all sorts combine into Regeneration pool. Regeneration  
    pool resets to 0 during a long rest. At the start of the round, all  
    creatures regain hit points from the their regeneration pool. This healing 
    is capped at 10% + 5% per constitution modifier of your maximum health.

    To rest and recover resources, a character can take a short or a long rest. Their duration and requirements are defined by character species. During a short rest, character recovers 25% + 5% per Constitution modifier of max HP. During a long rest, character recover all HP. 

    Healing and regeneration of all sorts combine into Regeneration pool which is reset to 0 during long rest. 
    At the start of the round, all creatures regain hit points from the their regeneration pool.
    This healing is capped at 10% + 5% per constitution modifier of your maximum health. 

### Ailments
    Bleeding: 
    target takes 1d4 internal damage at the beginning of the round. 
    Take 1d4 internal damage for each Focus spent. 
    Internal damage always damages regeneration pool first. 

    Burning: 
    target suffers fire damage at the beginning of the round.
    Flame has to be put out as a turn and it requires 1 Focus.
    Damage is based on target's size: 
    Small 1d4 / Medium 1d6 / Large 1d8 / Huge 1d10 / Gargantuan 1d12 

    Charmed: 
    Target is friendly towards the source of charm. 

    Dead: 
    When targets' hit points and regeneration pool both reach 0, it dies.
    
    Exposed: 
    Target of this ailment has their defenses lowered. Attacks against target gain advantage. 
    Exposure ends with start on target's next turn unless target is Incapacitated or Grappled. 
    In an instance of becoming exposed, nearby characters may take attacks of opportunity,
    against exposed target.

    Frightened: 
    Target has disadvantage on skill checks and accuracy checks while the source of its fear is within line of sight. 
    The target can’t willingly move closer to the source of its fear.
    
    Grappled: 
    Target is attached to a grappler. If grappler is at least one size bigger 
    than target, target's speed becomes 0. Condition ends if grappler becomes
    incapacitated. The condition also ends if target is displaced from the grappler. 

    Incapacitated: 
    Target cannot use focus, reactions and turns. 
    The target automatically fails Reflex Checks. 

    Incorporeal: 
    Target becomes immune to all physical damage, can pass through objects, 
    and has flying speed equal to their normal walking speed.
    Incorporeal targets are always vulnerable to Force damage. 

    Polarized: 
    Target is resistant to force damage, but vulnerable to lightning damage. 

    Paralyzed:
    Target is Incapacitated and Exposed.
    Any Attack that hits the target is a critical hit if the
    attacker is within 5 meters of the target. 

    Petrified: 
    Target is turned into stone-like matter. It becomes Paralyzed, 
    can’t move or speak and is unaware of its surroundings.
    Target becomes vulnerable to chemical damage, immune to biological damage,
    and resistant to all other forms of damage. 
    Poison or disease affecting target is not neutralized. 

    Prone:
    Melee attacks against prone target gain advantage, ranged attacks gain disadvantage. 

    Restrained: 
    Target automatically fails Reflex Checks,
    attackers have advantage on Accuracy Checks,
    target has disadvantage on Accuracy Checks. 

    Shocked: 
    Target cannot use reactions. 

    Stunned:
    Target is incapacitated, and can’t move. 
    The target automatically fails Reflex Throws. 
    Attack rolls against the target have advantage. 

    Surprised: 
    Target cannot act on their turn while surprised. Attacks against surprised targets gain advantage.
    Condition ends at the end of the first round of the encounter. 

    Unconscious:
    Target is incapacitated, can’t communicate, and is unaware of its surroundings.  
    The target drops whatever it is holding and falls prone.
    The target automatically fails Reflex Checks.
    Targets regain consciousness after regaining 20% of their HP. 

    Overwhelmed:
    Target is exposed and slowed. 

    Slowed: 
    Lose half of the movement speed.
    Reflex Checks gain disadvantage. 

    Sense Impairment (Hearing): 
    Target automatically fails all checks that require hearing. 

    Sense Impairment (Vision): 
    Target gains disadvantage on attacks and automatically fails all checks that require sight. 

    Weakness (Stacking): 
    Suffer -1 penalty to accuracy, weapon damage and all skill throws per stack.
